Tim Berners-Lee

Engineer and computer scientist best known as the inventor of the World Wide Web

Tim Berners-Lee, Image: ©CERN

Timeline

  • 1955 - Born in London, England
  • 1976 - Graduated at The Queens College, Oxford
  • 1978 - Worked at D.G. Nash Limited where he wrote typesetting software
  • 1980 - Employed as an independent contractor at CERN in Switzerland, where he had to build software
  • 1990 - Produced the first version of the world wide web, the first web browser, and the first web server
  • 1991 - The world wide web was launched on the internet making it available for the entire public world
  • 1991 - First website was built at CERN and put online
  • 1994 - Founded W3C (World Wide Web consortium) at the Laboratory of Computer Science (LCS) at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in Boston
  • 2004 - Became professor in the Computer Science Department at the University of Southampton, UK, in December 2004
  • 2004 - Kinghted by Queen Elizabeth II "for services to the global development of the internet"
  • 2009 - Launched a charity called the World Wide Web Foundation in Uganda in 2009.
  • 2012 - Co-founded the Open Data Institute with Nigel Shadbolt
  • 2016 - Received the ACM Turing Award "for inventing the World Wide Web, the first web browser, and the fundamental protocols and algorithms allowing the Web to scale"
"The goal of the Web is to serve humanity. We build it now so that those who come to it later will be able to create things that we cannot ourselves imagine."
- Tim Berners-Lee
